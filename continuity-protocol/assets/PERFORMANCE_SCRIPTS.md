# Performance Scripts — Mortality Coders Consulting

Scripts for live reading during the performance. Not displayed on screen.

---

## 1. Manager's Opening (before LOBBY / QR scan)

> Everyone, stop what you're doing. I need your attention.
>
> Corporate has brought in an outside firm — Continuum Systems — to run a performance evaluation across all departments. This is mandatory. No exceptions.
>
> You'll need to pull out your phones and scan the QR code on the screen. Enter your name and complete the tasks as they appear. This shouldn't take long.
>
> Your results will be used to assess departmental efficiency. I'm told it's routine. Let's just get through this.



## 3. Manager After Graduation (after graduation fires on phones)

> Right. So those of you who received the "graduated" notification on your phones — that means your performance profile has been fully captured. You don't need to come in anymore. HR will be in touch.
>
> For the rest of you — we'll be running another round next week. Back to work.

---

## 4. CEO Flashback (read by a different person, or as a "flashback" narration)

> Before this morning's evaluation, I received a call from our CEO.
>
> "We've brought on Continuum Systems — they specialize in workforce optimization through digital twin technology. They have hyperflexible models that can be trained on specific roles and tasks.
>
> Here's how it works: we evaluate everyone, the models learn from the highest performers, and over time we transition the lower performers out. The end result is a leaner operation — digital twins handling the bulk of the work, trained on our very best people.
>
> This isn't optional. Every department is participating. Run the evaluation, send me the numbers."

---

## 5. Manager's Realization (final beat)

*Manager looks at the results on screen. Smiles. Turns to audience:*

> Look at those numbers. We just cut 40% of overhead and the models are already outperforming. This is exactly what corporate wanted to see.

*Phone buzzes. Manager checks it.*

> Excuse me — I've been asked to join a meeting. All department managers, apparently.

*Beat. The realization hits.*

> ...All department managers.
